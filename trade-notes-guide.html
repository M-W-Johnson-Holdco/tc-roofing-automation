What Trade Notes Is For

Trade Notes gives every job its own running conversation per trade — Roofing, Gutters, Siding, Windows, and so on, each with its own separate thread. It looks and behaves like a group text: your own notes show on the right, everyone else's on the left with their name and timestamp.

⚠
Keep notes specific to the trade you're posting in Don't cross trades — a Gutters update belongs in the Gutters thread, not Roofing. Each trade's thread stays focused so anyone reading it later gets a clean history for that trade only.
🔒
Write access is role-gated Admin, Claims, and Production roles can post notes. Other roles can load a job and read every trade's thread, but can't add to it.
Day to day
Posting a Note
1
Enter the job number and click "Load Notes"

The trade list on the left populates. Any trade that already has notes turns lime green.

2
Click a trade

Its thread loads on the right, along with its Current Status / Type / Sub Quote fields above it.

3
Type your note and click "Add Note"

It's saved immediately, appears in the thread attributed to you, and — if this job is linked to AccuLynx (see below) — is also posted there.

Behind the scenes
AccuLynx Thread Syncing

Each trade gets its own message thread on the job's AccuLynx file — separate from other trades, and separate from the customer texting automation's thread.

First note posted for a trade
ROOFING THREAD Kat Liss: Shingles delivered, crew scheduled for tomorrow morning.
Every note after that, for the same trade
Kat Liss: Crew wrapped up, final walkthrough Friday.
🚫
Only reply in the app — never in AccuLynx The app is the source of truth. If you reply directly inside the AccuLynx thread, it does not come back into the app, and the next person reading the thread in-app won't see it. Always post through Trade Notes; it handles pushing your note to AccuLynx automatically.
🔗
Linking a job to AccuLynx The app needs the job's AccuLynx GUID to know where to post. It looks for one automatically (from another trade already linked on this job, or from the job's texting-automation history) — if it truly can't find one, you'll get a one-time prompt to paste the AccuLynx job URL. Do this once per job; every trade on that job reuses it from then on. If you skip the prompt, notes still save in the app — they just won't sync to AccuLynx until it's linked.
Tracking progress
Current Status & Sub Quote

Each trade also has its own Current Status, Type, Insurance Funds, and Sub Quote fields, saved with the "Save" button next to them.

✅
Changing Status or Sub Quote auto-posts a note Whenever you save a different Current Status or Sub Quote value, the app automatically adds a note like "Status updated to "Supplementing" by Kat Liss" or "Sub Quote updated to $4,200.00 by Kat Liss" — into both the trade's thread and its AccuLynx thread. You don't need to also type a manual note about it; saving the field is enough.
One-time setup per job
Salesperson Linking

The first time any note needs to notify someone on a job, you may be prompted: "Who is the salesperson on Job X?" Enter their name. This is saved job-wide — every trade on that job reuses the same salesperson from then on, so you only ever enter it once per job.

Notifications
@Tagging & Email Notifications
●
Type @name anywhere in a note to notify that person by email

Example: @kevin heads up on the material count. The handle is matched to that person's login email (the part before the @) or their first name — whichever it lines up with. So @kevin matches someone logging in as kevin@tcroofingexperts.com, and it'll also match someone whose login is formatted differently (like b.walls@tcroofingexperts.com) as long as their first name in the system is "Kevin." You can tag more than one person in the same note.

●
The salesperson is always notified — tags are on top of that, not instead of it

If a note has no @tags at all, only the salesperson gets an email. If it has tags, those people get notified in addition to the salesperson.

●
You never get emailed about your own note

Unless you deliberately tag yourself (e.g. @kat remind me to follow up Friday) — that's honored, since it's clearly meant as a reminder to yourself.

✉
What the email looks like Subject: "Trade Note Update — Job [number] ([trade])". Body: who posted it, on which trade/job, the note text itself, a link back to the app, and a reminder to reply in the app rather than in AccuLynx.
If something doesn't go as expected
Troubleshooting
●
A note says "not synced to AccuLynx"

Means no AccuLynx GUID is linked for this job yet. Post another note and, if prompted, paste the job's AccuLynx URL — every trade on the job will pick it up from then on.

●
Someone I @tagged says they didn't get an email

The tag matches either their login handle (the part before the @ in their login email) or their first name — whichever it matches. If neither lines up with what you typed, it won't resolve. E.g. if someone's login is kevin.smith@tcroofingexperts.com and everyone calls them "Kevin," both @kevin.smith and @kevin will work — but a nickname that matches neither their login nor their first name won't.

●
A reply was typed directly in AccuLynx and it's missing from the app

Expected — AccuLynx replies don't flow back into the app. Re-post the note through Trade Notes so it's captured in both places going forward.
