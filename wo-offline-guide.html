Why the steps below matter
Read This First — How Offline Mode Actually Works

Everything in this guide comes down to one fact: the app saves to your browser's local storage on that specific device before it ever tries to reach the internet. Understanding that makes every step below make sense instead of feeling like arbitrary ritual.

●
Local storage is tied to one device + one browser

It does not sync between your phone and laptop, and it does not sync between Chrome and Edge on the same laptop. Whatever device and browser you prep on is the only one that will have the offline data at the appointment.

●
Save always writes locally first, network second

Every 💾 Save button — on the Work Order tab or the Upgrades Commissions tab — writes to your device immediately, then tries to sync to the online sheet as a separate step afterward. If you're offline, that second part fails and shows an error message — but your local copy is already safe by the time you see it. Don't let that error message worry you.

●
Load tries online first, then falls back to your device automatically

Clicking 📂 Load always tries to reach the network first. If there's no signal, it fails silently in the background and pulls from your local cache instead — as long as that job's data is already on your device from Step 2 below.

●
Being signed in offline works too — but only if you've signed in successfully at least once

The app remembers your name and role locally, the same way it remembers Work Orders. If your device has no internet when the app loads, it waits a few seconds and then signs you back in from that cached login automatically (you'll see [offline] next to your name). But if you've never fully logged in on that device before, there's nothing to fall back to — you'll be stuck on the sign-in screen with no way to reach it.

💡
My take This is the one thing worth internalizing: everything downstream (Work Orders, upgrade options, even being logged in at all) depends on that device having done a successful online pass first. Offline mode isn't magic — it's a cache of things you already did while you had signal. Prep is really just "make sure the cache has what you need."
Do this once per device
1. One-Time Device Setup
1
Install Google Chrome on the device you'll actually carry to appointments

Use Chrome specifically for all of the steps below — and use it consistently. If you sometimes use Chrome and sometimes Edge on the same laptop, treat them as two completely separate devices as far as offline data goes.

2
Get online and sign in with your Google account

Load the Employee Portal, sign in, and confirm you see your name and role badge in the header before doing anything else. That successful login is what gets cached for the offline fallback described above.

3
Allow cookies / site data if Chrome asks

The app relies on your browser's local storage to hold everything offline. Blocking cookies or clearing site data for this site wipes out your cached login and any saved Work Orders — allow it to persist.

⚠
Don't clear browsing data on this device "Clear browsing data," "Clear cache," or a Chrome reset wipes local storage — which means your cached login and every saved Work Order and upgrade option disappear with it. If IT or a phone reset ever touches this device, redo the full setup and re-prep before your next offline appointment.
Do this for every job you'll visit
2. Prep the Work Order for Each Job
1
While still online, go to the Work Order tab

Type the job number into the Job Number field.

2
Click 💾 Save right away

This pulls in the pre-filled Work Order and writes it to your device's local storage immediately, keyed to that job number. This is the copy 📂 Load will fall back to later when you have no signal.

🗂
Multiple stops in one day? Local storage is keyed per job number, so repeat this for every job on your route before you leave — prepping one job does not prep the others.
Optional, but recommended
3. Prep Your 3 Upgrade Options
1
On the Upgrades Commissions tab, build out your three plans for this job

Use the same job number you saved the Work Order under in Step 2 — the options are matched up by job number.

2
Save each one as you finish it

Click 💾 Save Option 1, 💾 Save Option 2, and 💾 Save Option 3 for each plan. Each click saves to your device instantly and then syncs to the sheet in the background — same local-first behavior as the Work Order save.

3
Confirm they took, back on the Work Order tab

The Option 1 / Option 2 / Option 3 buttons next to the job number are greyed out until that job has a saved option to apply. If one is still greyed out after saving, it didn't save locally — try again before you lose signal.

Before you walk out the door
4. Lock It In Before You Head Out
1
Leave the app open on the Work Order tab and let the device sleep

Don't sign out and don't close Chrome entirely — just let the screen lock/sleep normally.

2
Do this a few hours ahead, not five minutes before you leave

Gives you a buffer to notice and fix a failed save (bad job number, missed Option save, etc.) while you still have signal to correct it.

✅
My take — do a dry run once The first time you rely on this, prep a job as above, then turn on Airplane Mode and try 📂 Load and Option 1 before you actually need them at a live appointment. Takes two minutes and turns "I think this works" into "I know this works." Turn Airplane Mode back off afterward.
🔋
My take — charge it Sleep still drains battery over several hours. Plug the device in (or top it off) as part of this step, especially if the appointment is later in the day.
No signal, live with the homeowner
5. At the Appointment (No Signal)
1
Wake the device, type the job number, click 📂 Load

This loads the pre-filled Work Order you saved back in Step 2, straight from local storage.

2
Click whichever Option matches what you and the homeowner land on

This syncs that option's materials and pricing on top of the Work Order already loaded — it is not reloading the Work Order itself, so there's no risk of losing what's already on screen. You'll get a warning message when you do this; that warning is just describing the sync, not a problem.

Finishing the document
6. Wrapping Up & Signatures
1
Save as you go

You'll see ⚠ Unsaved changes — tap Save to keep your work appear as you fill in notes and choices. Get in the habit of clicking 💾 Save every few fields rather than waiting until the very end.

2
Save again immediately after signatures are collected

Still offline, so this save is local-only — but it means you never have to start the document over if something interrupts you right after.

3
Export and save the completed Work Order to the device

Use Export as Flattened PDF once everything is signed and saved. This is what you'll upload to AccuLynx.

ℹ
If Save shows an error while you're still offline That's expected — it's the online sheet sync failing, not the save itself. Your local copy already wrote to the device before that network step ran. Don't re-enter anything; just keep going and reconcile once you're back in service (Step 7).
Once you're back in signal range
7. Back in Service Area — Reconcile Everything
1
Click 💾 Save on the Work Order tab again

Now that you have signal, this pushes your finalized, signed Work Order up to the online sheet, so it's there if anyone needs to reference or edit it later.

2
Upload the exported PDF to AccuLynx

Use the flattened PDF you exported in Step 6.

3
Submit your Upgrades Commissions tab to the payroll sheet

Reflect whatever the final decision was at the appointment — this is how you see your own commission, and how payroll admin can see it too.

If something doesn't go as expected
Troubleshooting
●
"No work order found for job X" while offline

Step 2 wasn't completed for that job on this specific device before you left — there's nothing local to fall back to. Keep a paper or photo backup of key job details as a last resort for this scenario.

●
App shows the Google sign-in screen instead of loading straight in

Means this device never completed a full successful login before (see Step 1) — the offline fallback has nothing cached to restore. There's no way to sign in without signal, so this has to be fixed before you leave the office, not at the appointment.

●
Option 1/2/3 button still greyed out at the appointment

That option never saved locally on this device. Apply whichever ones did save, and finish the rest manually.

Recap
Quick Pre-Flight Checklist
  • Chrome installed, cookies/site data allowed, on the exact device you're bringing
  • Signed in and full name/role visible in the header at least once on this device
  • Job number entered and 💾 Save clicked for every job on today's route
  • All 3 upgrade options saved for each job, and Option buttons confirmed not greyed out
  • Device charged, left signed in, put to sleep a few hours before departure — not last minute
  • (Optional) Did an Airplane Mode dry run at least once to confirm offline load actually works
